The YouTube video, “Spanish Differences Between Spain, Mexico, and Argentina” displays Spanish speakers from different spanish-speaking countries including Spain, Mexico, and Argentina. All 3 of these women speak the same Spanish language but they speak different dialects. This means they use different words from the same standard language to convey the same meanings. For example, the word car in Mexico is known as “caro”, in Argentina it’s “auto”, and in Spain it’s “coche.” For further examples please watch the video.
